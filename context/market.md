# Attio: market and category

> Source: attio.com + public sources, June 2026.

## Category

CRM (customer relationship management). Attio's twist on the category is "AI-native CRM," staking a position against both legacy CRMs and flexible-but-not-CRM tools.

## Where they play

- **Segment:** startups, builders, high-growth tech companies, and VC firms. Bottom-up, PLG motion with a free tier.
- **Buyer:** founders, early GTM hires, RevOps, and operators at startups who want a CRM that bends to their data model rather than forcing them into a rigid one.
- **Motion:** product-led (free tier), with seat-based expansion. Land with a builder, expand across the team.

## Why this is a good audit example for performance marketers

- **Crowded category, feature parity:** CRM is one of the most contested categories in software. "AI-native" is now claimed by nearly everyone, so the differentiation is under pressure. That makes the positioning audit produce real debt and real white space.
- **Clear competitive set:** the alternatives are well known, so the audit's competitive scoring is concrete.
- **Visible ads:** Attio runs paid and design-forward creative, so the "compare today's ads to the foundations" move has material to work with.

## The teaching point

Attio has strong positioning. The audit is not about catching them out. It is about showing how, even for a sharp brand, the inputs a paid team inherits (a saved audience, a website headline, last quarter's ad copy) can drift from the foundations. The skills rebuild those inputs from the ground up, fast.
