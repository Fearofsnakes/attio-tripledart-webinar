# Attio: voice and tone (as observed)

> How Attio talks, from their site and public copy, June 2026. Feeds the messaging-hierarchy run so the output sounds like Attio, not generic SaaS.

## Tone

- Confident, clean, builder-to-builder. Speaks to operators who know what a CRM is and are frustrated by the legacy ones.
- Product-forward and design-led. Lets the interface and the data model do a lot of the talking.
- Restrained. Not hype-heavy. Lets named customers and short testimonials carry credibility ("feels like magic," "the next generation of CRM").

## Vocabulary they use

- "AI-native," "data model," "custom objects," "workflows," "context layer"
- "Startups and builders," "modern go-to-market"
- "Unify customer data," "automate," "build custom solutions"

## What to avoid when writing in their voice

- Generic CRM cliches ("manage your relationships," "close more deals") that any competitor could say
- Over-claiming AI magic without a concrete mechanism
- Enterprise-speak. Their audience is builders, not procurement.
