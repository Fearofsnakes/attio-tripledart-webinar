# Attio — Positioning Audit Report

> Output of the `positioning-audit` skill (mode B: audit-only), run live on the Attio context (TripleDart webinar, June 2026). Audience pillar = the ICP from `icp.md`. Audited on Attio's **flexible-data-model wedge**, not the AI-native claim. Pre-built fallback preserved at `positioning.prebuilt-backup.md`.

## THE 4 PILLARS

### 1. Use Case & Category
- **Category:** CRM, entered through the wedge **"the most flexible, customizable data model in CRM."** Flexibility is the position; AI-native demotes to a feature riding on top of the model.
- **Use case:** the startup system of record that **bends to how you actually sell** — custom objects, your schema, your workflows — instead of a rigid pipeline you didn't design.

### 2. Audience
Per `icp.md`: builder-led B2B startups, 10–80 employees, seed→Series B, at the moment the spreadsheet breaks and a technical first-GTM-hire picks the CRM before procurement exists. Demo-averse, self-serve, configures it themselves.

### 3. Competitive Alternative
- **Status quo:** spreadsheet / Notion DB — free and flexible, becomes a graveyard at scale.
- **Direct:** HubSpot (rigid object model, AI bolted on), Salesforce (bloated, needs an admin — the thing they fled).
- **Adjacent:** Notion/Airtable (flexible but *not a CRM*), Folk/Pipedrive (simple = shallow), Clay (AI GTM work, not a system of record).
- **The squeeze:** Notion owns "flexible," Salesforce owns "real CRM." **Attio's only defensible ground is the overlap neither can hold: flexible AND a real CRM.**

### 4. Differentiation
> For builder-led startups (10–80) who've outgrown the spreadsheet but refuse to be bent by a rigid CRM, Attio provides a fully customizable data model on a real CRM backbone (sync, enrichment, reporting, sequences), configurable without an admin — which means they set it up the way they sell, in an afternoon, and it still scales at 200 people.

**Competitor Swap Test:**
- "Salesforce… flexible like Notion, set up in an afternoon without an admin" → ❌ fails. Attio wins.
- "Notion… a real CRM backbone, native sync/enrichment/sequences" → ❌ fails. Attio wins.
- "HubSpot… a fully customizable data model" → ⚠️ partial (HubSpot has gated custom objects). Attio wins on degree, not kind.

**Verdict:** differentiation is real against Salesforce and Notion (the squeeze) — ownable. Softer against HubSpot. Holds **only if Attio leads with the both/and overlap.** Lead with "flexible" alone → looks like Notion. Lead with "AI-native" → looks like everyone.

## POSITIONING STATEMENT
> Attio is the CRM for builder-led startups who've outgrown the spreadsheet but won't be boxed in by a legacy CRM. Unlike Salesforce or HubSpot (which bend you to their model) or Notion and Airtable (flexible, but not a real CRM), Attio gives you a fully customizable data model on a real CRM backbone — so you set it up the way you sell, in an afternoon, without an admin, and it still scales at 200 people.

## POSITIONING DEBT FLAGS
- 🚩 **Competitive mimicry (most critical):** leads with "AI-native," the exact frame Salesforce/HubSpot/Zoho use → bids on the most crowded, least-ownable axis → higher CPCs, "how are you different from HubSpot's AI?" objections.
- 🚩 **Feature-first drift:** "flexible data model / custom objects / AI agents" are capabilities; the buyer outcome ("set it up the way you sell, scale without an admin") is buried → clicks from feature-shoppers, not pain-feelers.
- 🚩 **Buried wedge / clarity gap:** the one ownable thing (flexible AND real CRM) needs a full sentence and isn't above the fold → the position that lowers CAC is the one prospects never see first.

> **Performance-marketer payoff:** every flag is fixable in ad copy + landing headline, no product change. The media budget is currently bidding on the one dimension Attio can't win; move it to the one they own and the same spend converts better.

## REPOSITIONING RECOMMENDATION

```
Current (as marketed):  "AI-native CRM for startups and builders."
Proposed:               "The CRM that bends to how you sell — flexible like a Notion doc,
                         real like a CRM, set up in an afternoon without an admin."

What changes:
  Category:        Same (CRM); wedge shifts AI-native → flexible data model + real CRM
  Audience:        Sharpened — builder-led startups, 10–80, first-GTM-hire moment
  Alternative:     Reframed — lead against Notion AND Salesforce (the squeeze)
  Differentiation: Both/and overlap becomes the headline; AI-native demotes to proof

Why now:    Ownability 2/5 because the lead claim is copyable; the wedge already exists
            in-product (durability 4/5). AI-native parity hit in 2026 — that window closed.
Risk:       Some equity on the AI-native headline. Mitigate: AI-native becomes the PROOF
            of flexibility ("the AI works because the data model is yours"), not a rival claim.
Validation: (1) A/B landing H1 AI-native vs bends-to-how-you-sell, watch signup-start.
            (2) Two equal-budget ad sets, AI-native vs both/and, compare CAC over 2 weeks.
            (3) Review 5 win calls: did buyers cite "AI" or "it fit how we work"?
```

## STAKEHOLDER SUMMARY (copy-paste ready)
> Attio's positioning scores 3.4/5. The biggest gap is Ownability (2/5): they market a copyable claim (AI-native) when they own a defensible one (flexible data model + real CRM, admin-free). They're differentiated against Salesforce and Notion on the dimension the builder ICP cares about most — but the messaging leads with the crowded axis. We recommend shifting the lead from "AI-native CRM" to the both/and squeeze, demoting AI to proof. A messaging fix, not a product fix — testable in two ad sets and one landing A/B before committing.
