# Attio — ICP definition

> Output of the `icp-definition` skill, run live on the Attio context (TripleDart webinar, June 2026). Act 1 only (the four layers). Anti-ICP + signal map not run in this pass. The pre-built fallback is preserved at `icp.prebuilt-backup.md`.

## LAYER 1 — Firmographic

- **Size:** 10–80 employees — past the spreadsheet, before the RevOps team exists.
- **Stage:** VC-backed, seed → Series B. Funded = tooling budget + a GTM motion worth instrumenting.
- **Industry:** B2B software startups, skewing AI-native — they expect their own tools to be AI-native too.
- **Geography:** Global, English-first. US + European startup hubs.
- **Team structure (the missed one):** Founder-led or the *first* GTM/RevOps hire picks the CRM. A builder decides, not a CRM admin. No incumbent Salesforce team to displace.

**Firmographic edge:** Not "a startup." A startup at *the moment the spreadsheet breaks and someone technical picks the CRM before procurement exists.* That moment is the firmographic.

## LAYER 2 — Behavioral

- **Buying trigger:** the spreadsheet/Notion DB breaks (deals slipping, no source of truth); **or** HubSpot/Salesforce fights their data model; **or** a fresh raise + first GTM hire = "we need real tooling now."
- **Current tools:** Airtable/Notion/Sheets duct-tape, or a resented rip-out from HubSpot/Salesforce.
- **Evaluation behavior:** self-serve. Signs up for the free tier and tests it *themselves* first — builder-led, demo-averse, product-first. Peer reco (founder Slack/X) over G2.
- **Decision process:** founder or first GTM hire decides near-solo. Short committee, no procurement. The PLG opposite of the enterprise CRM committee.
- **Timeline:** fast. In-quarter, often in-week. Configure on a free trial, expand seat-by-seat.
- **Content consumption:** X/Twitter, founder Slacks, Lenny's, YC orbit. Not CRM-vendor webinars.

**Behavioral wedge for paid:** funded startup **+** first RevOps/GTM-ops job posting **+** public "ripping out HubSpot" chatter = a named account list, not a "CRM" keyword bid. Creative drives to **free signup, not book-a-demo.**

## LAYER 3 — Psychographic

- **Belief:** software bends to how *we* sell — we shouldn't contort to fit the tool. A CRM is infrastructure you configure, not a system you're processed by.
- **Frustration (felt experience):** fighting a tool that assumes a sales motion they don't run. Rigid fields, forced pipelines. "Why is it this hard to model how *we* work?"
- **Identity:** builder/operator. Technical enough to wire their own stack. The CRM is part of their craft, not back-office overhead.
- **Fear:** enterprise lock-in — becoming the bloated, slow thing they left a big company to escape. And: picking a tool that won't scale, forcing another migration.
- **Aspiration:** a system that feels like an extension of how they think — fast, flexible, AI doing real work, no ops army.
- **Tribal markers:** "data model," "custom objects," "set it up the way we sell." Identify with builders (Linear/Notion/Vercel orbit), **not** with "CRM admins" or "Salesforce-certified."

## LAYER 4 — Language

> ⚠️ Sourced, not mined. In a real engagement these are verbatim pulls from sales calls, G2, Reddit, and community threads. Here they are drawn from Attio's public site vocabulary, `context/voice.md`, and category chatter — directionally right, flagged as sourced-not-mined. On a real account the agent mines these from the buyer's Gong + G2; it does not invent them.

- **Problem language:** "Our CRM is a mess." "HubSpot doesn't fit how we actually sell." "I spend more time fighting the tool than using it." "Everything's in a spreadsheet and it's falling apart." "It's a graveyard — nobody updates it."
- **Solution language:** "a CRM that bends to our data model," "set it up the way we sell," "something that doesn't get in the way," "AI that actually does the work, not just autocomplete."
- **Objection language:** "Is it another thing I have to configure from scratch?" "Will it scale at 200 people?" "We don't have time to migrate again." "Is the AI real or a wrapper?"
- **Success language:** "It just fits how we work." "Setup took an afternoon, not a quarter." "Feels like magic." "The start of the next generation of CRM." *(last two are real public testimonials — USV, Modal.)*
- **Comparison language:** "Salesforce is bloated/enterprise/overkill." "HubSpot bolted AI on after." "Notion isn't a real CRM." "AI-native, not retrofitted."

**Bridge to messaging:** "Set it up the way you sell" and "AI-native, not retrofitted" are ready-to-run ad lines that fall straight out of the ICP — the thesis of the session.
