# Attio — positioning audit

> Output of the `positioning-audit` skill, run on the Attio context. Pre-built for the webinar demo. June 2026.

## Act 1 — The four pillars

- **Category:** AI-native CRM.
- **Audience:** startups and builders running a modern go-to-market.
- **Competitive alternative:** legacy CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot) and flexible-but-not-a-CRM tools (Notion, Airtable).
- **Differentiation:** AI built into the data model from inception, plus the most customizable data model in CRM.

**Positioning statement:** For startups and builders who have outgrown rigid CRMs, Attio is the AI-native CRM whose data model bends to how you actually sell, so AI can do real work on top of it.

## Act 3 — The fix: white space

The crowded middle is "AI-native." Everyone is there. The space Attio can own:

1. **"The CRM your AI agents can actually use."** The data model as the thing that makes AI useful, not the AI label itself.
2. **"Seed to IPO."** Scales with the company (their own ad already gestures at this) — a durability claim rivals can't easily match.
3. **"Your data model, not theirs."** Flexibility reframed as control, the builder's emotional hook.

**For ad angles:** stop bidding the "AI-native" auction everyone is in. Lead with the data model and the agent-ready outcome, where there is air.
