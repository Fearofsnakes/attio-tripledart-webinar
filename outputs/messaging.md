# Attio — messaging hierarchy

> Output of the `messaging-hierarchy` skill, run on the Attio context. Pre-built for the webinar demo. This is the source every ad in `ad-variations.html` ladders to. June 2026.

## The five-layer stack

**01 · POV**
A CRM retrofitted with AI will always lose to one built AI-native. The data model is the moat, not the AI label.

**02 · Value proposition**
The AI-native CRM with a data model flexible enough to match how you actually sell, so AI can do real work on top of it.

**03 · Benefits**
1. **It bends to you.** Custom objects and your schema, not a rigid template.
2. **AI does the work.** Research, enrichment, and workflows run for you, not just data entry.
3. **Fast, and it scales.** Live in an afternoon, and it grows with you from seed to IPO.

**04 · Proof points**
- 80,000+ startups run on Attio.
- Granola, Modal, Railway, Listen Labs, Wispr Flow, Union Square Ventures.
- "The start of the next generation of CRM." (Modal)

**05 · Features**
Custom objects · AI research agents · workflow engine · enrichment · email + calendar sync · reporting.

## Traceability check
Every benefit ties to a feature and pays off the POV. POV (AI-native, data-model moat) → VP (flexible data model AI can act on) → Benefits (bends / does work / scales) → Proof (80k + logos) → Features (objects, agents, workflows).

## The 60-second pitch
"Most CRMs were built before AI and bolted it on later. Attio was built AI-native, with the most flexible data model in the category, so it bends to how your team actually sells. That structure is what lets AI do real work on top of it: researching accounts, enriching records, running your workflows. You're live in an afternoon, and it scales with you from seed to IPO. 80,000 startups already made the switch."
